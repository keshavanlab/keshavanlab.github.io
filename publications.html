First Episode Psychosis

Psychosis is a severe mental disorder in which thought and emotions are impaired to the extent that a person separates from reality and experiences delusions and hallucinations. First episode psychosis is the first time a person experiences psychosis symptoms. Click on the icon to read a few publications about first-episode psychosis.

Imaging

These publications discuss techniques on magnetic resonance imaging (MRI) and magnetic resonance spectroscopy. Magnetic resonance imaging (MRI) is an imaging technique that utilizes magnetic fields and radio waves to form images of the body, specifically the brain in this laboratory's case. For neuroimaging, MRI is the better imaging technique for it allows a better contrast between grey and white matter in the brain. Magnetic resonance spectroscopy is a technique uses magnetic resonance to provide greater depth of information regarding the structure, dynamics, and chemical environment of molecules. Click on the to read more about imaging techniques.

Cognitive Remediation

Cognitive Remediation Therapy (CRT) refers to a set of cognitive tasks to improve cognitive function in patients with mental disorders such as schizophrenia. Cognitive remediation may help to ameliorate the neurobiological damage caused by these mental disorders. Click on the icon to read a few publications about cognitive remediation.

Psychosis High Risk

The clinical high-risk state for psychosis is described in three states: "at-risk mental state" (ARMS), prodromal (period between initial symptoms and full development of disorder), and "ultra-high-risk" (UHR). The risk of schizophrenia is correlated with the type of relationship and number of affected of relatives. Click on the icon to read more about high-risk studies and schizophrenia.

Biomarkers

Biomarkers is an observable and quantifiable characteristic of biological processes that can be measured and evaluated. Biomarkers are significant in strengthening understanding of the relationship between biological processes and clinical observations, and with this understanding, the wide array of treatments for diseases can be expanded upon.
