Cognitive Remediation Therapy (CRT) refers to a set of cognitive tasks to improve cognitive function in patients with mental disorders such as schizophrenia. Imaging

These publications discuss techniques on magnetic resonance imaging (MRI) and magnetic resonance spectroscopy. Magnetic resonance imaging (MRI) is an imaging technique that utilizes magnetic fields and radio waves to form images of the body, specifically the brain in this laboratory's case. For neuroimaging, MRI is the better imaging technique for it allows a better contrast between grey and white matter in the brain. Magnetic resonance spectroscopy is a technique uses magnetic resonance to provide greater depth of information regarding the structure, dynamics, and chemical environment of molecules. The clinical high-risk state for psychosis is described in three states: "at-risk mental state" (ARMS), prodromal (period between initial symptoms and full development of disorder), and "ultra-high-risk" (UHR).
